Mission & History

FIERCE (Fostering Inclusion by Engaging in Real-World Computing Education) is an inclusive Residential RAP for incoming computer science and informatics majors. Designed to welcome students who may feel newer to studying computing, FIERCE provides opportunities for academic and social support to foster and sustain professional support networks. FIERCE honors women's contributions to computing and welcomes all students including those from marginalized communities in computing: Women, LGBTQ+, BIPOC, and others.

Since launching in 2019, the program has grown into a welcoming community where students build skills, share experiences, and support one another throughout their academic journey.

Our Team

Benefits of Joining

  • Mentorship from experienced peer leaders in the CICS community.
  • Living on the same residential floor with fellow FIERCE students.
  • A specialized first-year seminar designed for FIERCE students.
  • One-on-one tutoring and academic support throughout your first year.
  • Networking opportunities with industry professionals and the broader tech community.
  • Career exploration and volunteer experiences that help students discover new paths.
  • Community-building outings and trips to Boston tech companies like Akamai and MathWorks.

Requirements

  • An interest in learning computing and participating in community activities.
  • Openness to collaboration and peer support.
  • Willingness to engage in workshops and program events.
